Highlights

Snorkeling
Garuae Pass — North Pass Drift

The world's widest atoll pass (1.6 km). Drift snorkel through warm crystal-clear water alongside manta rays, dolphins, and reef sharks. Safe for older kids with a guide.

Diving
Tumakohua — South Pass Wall of Sharks

Legendary dive site where 300+ grey reef sharks patrol a narrow channel. Adults-only experience; one of the top 10 dive sites on Earth.

Kayaking
Pink Sand Motu Expedition

Paddle across the lagoon to uninhabited motus with pink-sand beaches. Pack a picnic and snorkel gear — perfect family adventure.

Culture
Rotoava Village Walking Tour

Stroll through the main village to see the 19th-century coral church, meet local pearl farmers, and browse shell jewelry. A gentle intro to Tuamotu life.

Nature
Bioluminescent Night Lagoon Tour

Wade or kayak into the lagoon at night and watch the water glow electric blue. A magical experience for kids — bring waterproof cameras.

Cultural Connections

Tahitian Black Pearl Farm Visit

Tour a working pearl farm in the lagoon. Learn how Pinctada margaritifera oysters produce the famous black pearls of the Tuamotus. Kids can hold oysters and see the grafting process.

Historic Coral Church of Rotoava

Visit the 19th-century Protestant church built entirely from coral blocks. One of the oldest standing structures in the Tuamotus and still active for Sunday services.

UNESCO Biosphere Reserve

Fakarava is one of 7 atolls in the Commune of Fakarava Biosphere Reserve. Learn about marine conservation and sustainable fishing practices from local guides.

Traditional Net Fishing

Join local fishermen in the lagoon for traditional "stone fishing" — an ancient Polynesian technique where the community works together to herd fish into shallow water.

Tuamotu Weaving Workshop

Learn pandanus leaf weaving from village artisans. Make your own hat or basket in a hands-on session — a beautiful souvenir and a genuine cultural exchange.

Highlights

Adventure
Cloud 9 Floating Bar

A two-level floating platform bar in the middle of the ocean between Mamanuca islands. Swim, lounge, and eat wood-fired pizza. Boat transfer from Denarau; kids love the floating trampolines.

Culture
Traditional Village Visit & Kava Ceremony

Visit an authentic Fijian village, participate in a sevusevu kava ceremony, and watch meke dance performances. An immersive cultural experience that kids will remember forever.

Island Day
Monuriki Island (Cast Away Island)

Day trip to the island where "Cast Away" was filmed. Snorkel with sea turtles, hike the volcanic ridge, and beach picnic. Boat transfer ~45 min from Denarau.

Snorkeling
Mamanuca Reef Snorkel Safari

Multi-stop snorkel tour across the reef system. See clownfish, parrotfish, and reef sharks in shallow, calm water. Guides carry floats for younger kids.

Relaxation
Sabeto Hot Springs & Mud Pool

Natural geothermal hot springs and volcanic mud pools near Nadi. Slather on warm mud, let it dry, then rinse in the hot springs. A huge hit with kids of all ages.

Cultural Connections

Kava Ceremony & Village Stay

Present yaqona (kava root) to the village chief in a traditional sevusevu ceremony. Share stories, drink kava, and learn about communal village governance and Fijian social structures.

Garden of the Sleeping Giant

Walk through Raymond Burr's former private orchid garden, now home to 2,000+ orchid species. The lush rainforest setting is mesmerizing — peaceful and educational for all ages.

Sri Siva Subramaniya Temple

The largest Hindu temple in the Southern Hemisphere, right in Nadi. Vibrant Dravidian architecture, colorful murals, and a window into Fiji's significant Indo-Fijian heritage.

Fijian Meke Dance Performance

Watch traditional meke — a sacred dance combining chanting, drumming, and choreography that tells Fijian legends. Often performed at resort cultural nights; kids can join in.

Firewalking Ceremony at Beqa Island

Witness the legendary vilavilairevo — Beqa islanders walk barefoot across white-hot stones. A powerful and ancient tradition unique to this Fijian clan.

Highlights

Lagoon Cruise
One Foot Island Lagoon Cruise

The quintessential Aitutaki experience. Full-day cruise across the turquoise lagoon to One Foot Island (Tapuaetai). Get your passport stamped, snorkel, and BBQ lunch on a sand bar.

Snorkeling
Giant Clam Sanctuary

Snorkel over a protected area of giant tridacna clams — some over 100 years old, with iridescent blue and green mantles. Shallow water, perfect for kids.

Fishing
Deep Sea Fishing Charter

Chase yellowfin tuna, mahi-mahi, and wahoo outside the reef. Half-day charters available with kid-friendly captains. Your catch gets cooked at your resort that night.

Hike
Maunga Pu Summit Hike

Short hike (30 min) to the top of Aitutaki's highest point (124m). Panoramic 360° views of the entire lagoon and all 15 motus. Best at sunset. Manageable for ages 8+.

Beach
Honeymoon Island (Motu Akaiamiil)

Tiny uninhabited islet with the whitest sand in the Cooks. Arrive by kayak or water taxi. Pure Robinson Crusoe vibes — bring your own shade and snorkel gear.

Cultural Connections

Cook Islands Christian Church (CICC)

Attend a Sunday service with soaring a cappella hymns in Cook Islands Māori. The coral-stone churches are stunning and the singing is world-class — often called the "Polynesian Vatican choir."

Marae Ruins — Ancient Stone Temples

Explore ancient Polynesian ceremonial platforms (marae) scattered across the island. Your guide explains their role in pre-Christian Cook Islands society — navigation, sacrifice, and chieftainship.

Tivaevae Quilting Workshop

Learn the art of tivaevae — intricately hand-stitched appliqué quilts that are a symbol of Cook Islands women's craft. These heirlooms take months to complete. A unique hands-on experience.

Ura Dance Performance & Lesson

Watch the rhythmic hip-swaying ura dance — Cook Islands' signature art form. Audience participation is encouraged; kids always steal the show. Held at resorts and community halls.

Coconut Husking & Weaving Demo

Learn to husk a coconut with a machete, grate the meat, and extract fresh cream. Then weave coconut fronds into baskets and plates. A staple of Cook Islands daily life.

Highlights

Swimming
To Sua Ocean Trench

Samoa's most iconic sight — a 30-meter deep turquoise swimming hole connected to the ocean by a lava tunnel. Descend via a rickety ladder into gin-clear water. Magical for older kids; carry the 4-year-old down.

Beach
Lalomanu Beach

Consistently rated Samoa's most beautiful beach — blindingly white sand, swaying palms, and warm turquoise water. Beach fale (huts) for rent right on the sand. The kind of beach that makes you whisper "wow."

Nature
Papapapaitai Falls Lookout

Samoa's highest waterfall plunges 100m into a rainforest gorge. Easy drive to the viewing platform — a jaw-dropping sight, especially after rain. The road itself is gorgeous.

Culture
Robert Louis Stevenson Museum

The "Treasure Island" author's last home, now a beautifully preserved colonial museum. Hike to his hilltop tomb for panoramic views of Apia. Great for bookish kids (and parents).

Adventure
Alofaaga Blowholes (Savai'i)

Powerful ocean blowholes that shoot seawater 20m into the air. Locals throw coconuts into the holes and watch them launch skyward. Kids will be mesmerized. Worth the ferry to Savai'i.

Cultural Connections

Fia Fia Night — Song, Dance & Fire Knife

Samoa's legendary cultural evening. Watch siva dances, listen to multi-part harmony singing, then gasp as the fire knife (siva afi) dancer whips flaming blades. Held at most resorts weekly.

Samoan Tatau (Tattoo) Culture

Learn about pe'a (men's full-body tattoo) and malu (women's) — sacred marks of identity, status, and service. Visit a tufuga ta tatau (master tattoo artist) studio in Apia for a respectful cultural immersion.

Sunday Toona'i — Family Feast

Experience the Samoan Sunday ritual: attend a white-clad church service (incredible singing), then join a family for toona'i — the massive Sunday umu (earth-oven) feast with taro, palusami, and roast pork.

Samoa Cultural Village (Free)

Open-air museum in the center of Apia, run by elders. Free demonstrations of siapo (bark cloth) making, 'ava ceremony, coconut weaving, and traditional cooking. Runs every weekday.

Vailima — Robert Louis Stevenson's Estate

The "Tusitala" (teller of tales) lived his final years in this colonial mansion. Now a museum with original furnishings and family photos. Hike to his hilltop tomb inscribed with his poem "Requiem."

Highlights

Snorkeling
Tiputa Pass Dolphin Drift

Drift snorkel through Tiputa Pass alongside pods of spinner dolphins, manta rays, and harmless reef sharks. The current does the work — just float and watch the show. Safe with a guide for ages 8+.

Wine
Vin de Tahiti — Coral Wine Estate

The world's only coral-atoll vineyard. Tour the vines growing in crushed coral soil, taste the unique Blanc de Corail, and buy bottles as gifts. Adults love it; kids enjoy the oddity.

Lagoon
Blue Lagoon Excursion

Day trip to a lagoon-within-a-lagoon — a secluded natural pool with shallow, impossibly blue water and baby blacktip sharks swimming at your feet. BBQ lunch on a motu. A highlight for the whole family.

Diving
Avatoru Pass Wall Dive

Dramatic underwater wall teeming with grey reef sharks, napoleonfish, and eagle rays. One of the top 5 dives in French Polynesia. Adults-only (certified divers).

Nature
Île aux Récifs — Fossilized Coral Island

Boat to a surreal island of jagged, fossilized coral pinnacles rising from the lagoon like an alien landscape. Photograph the otherworldly formations and spot nesting seabirds. Unique to Rangiroa.

Cultural Connections

Black Pearl Farm Tour

Visit a lagoon-side pearl farm and watch the delicate grafting process. Rangiroa produces some of the finest black pearls in the world — learn to judge color, luster, and shape. Kids can hold live oysters.

Gauguin's Pearl — Art Gallery

Small gallery celebrating Polynesian art and the artistic legacy of Gauguin's time in the Tuamotus. Local artists display paintings, carvings, and tapa cloth works. Intimate and peaceful.

Traditional Polynesian Navigation

Learn from local guides how ancient Polynesians navigated thousands of miles using stars, currents, and bird patterns. Rangiroa's remote location makes the night sky spectacular for stargazing sessions.

Avatoru Village Walking Tour

Stroll through Rangiroa's main village — visit the century-old coral church, the tiny school, and the copra-drying racks. See how a remote atoll community lives day-to-day.

Coconut Crab Night Walk

After dark, venture to the motu edges with a local guide to spot massive coconut crabs (kaveu) — the world's largest land arthropods. A thrilling and educational wildlife encounter for adventurous families.
